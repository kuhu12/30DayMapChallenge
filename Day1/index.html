Is SF a Bike-Friendly City?

Bike Share Stations in San Francisco

In the Bay Area, bike share options have always been expanding, and the San Francisco Municipal Transportation Agency (SFMTA) is responsible for this task in the city and county of San Francisco.

Bikeshare is a great option for quick and affordable stand-alone trips to get around the city. It was such a useful transportation option for the busiest travel times that Bay Area residents and commuters face. However, due to this pandemic, the biking industry has seen a sudden spike in sales. But are cities ready for this change?

The map is showing 260 bike share stations in the city of San Francisco. Terry Francois Blvd at Warriors Way has the highest dock count of 42 while Glen Park BART station has the second-highest dock count. The most surprising thing on the map was the Sunset District, which has no bike-share stations. In addition, West Twin Peak and Marina Pacific Heights neighborhoods have very few or no bike stations.
